# Third-Party and Vendor Risk Under the CBN Framework

**Reference:** CBN Risk-Based Cybersecurity Framework, Third-Party Risk Provisions
**Applies To:** Commercial banks, merchant banks, non-interest banks, and Payment Service Banks
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## Why This Matters

The CBN framework explicitly places vendor and outsourcing risk in scope. A breach at your core banking software vendor, your cloud provider, or a payment processing partner is treated as your risk, not a problem you can point away from during a supervisory review.

---

## Vendor Risk Requirements

| Requirement | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| Vendor inventory | A complete, current list of every third party with access to systems or customer data |
| Pre-onboarding assessment | Security due diligence conducted before, not after, a vendor relationship begins |
| Contractual security clauses | Data protection, breach notification, and audit rights written into every vendor contract |
| Ongoing monitoring | Vendor risk is reassessed periodically, not just at the start of the relationship |
| Outsourcing alignment | Arrangements are assessed against CBN's specific outsourcing guidelines, a related but distinct regulatory requirement |

---

## Building a Vendor Risk Tiering Model

Not every vendor carries the same risk. A practical tiering approach:

| Tier | Criteria | Example | Review Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (Critical) | Direct access to core banking systems or customer financial data | Core banking software provider, payment switch | Quarterly review, continuous monitoring where possible |
| Tier 2 (High) | Access to sensitive but non-core systems or limited customer data | CRM provider, marketing platform with customer data | Semi-annual review |
| Tier 3 (Standard) | No direct access to sensitive systems or data | Office supplies, non-data-touching services | Annual review |

## Fourth-Party Risk: The Layer Most Institutions Miss

Your vendor's own vendors are increasingly part of your risk surface. A breach at your core banking vendor's cloud infrastructure provider is still your incident from a regulatory and customer perspective.

**Practical steps:**
- Ask Tier 1 vendors to disclose their own critical subcontractors
- Request evidence that your vendor conducts due diligence on their vendors
- Include fourth-party disclosure requirements in vendor contracts going forward

---

## Common Gap

Institutions often have strong initial vendor due diligence but weak ongoing monitoring. A vendor assessed as low risk two years ago may have since expanded their access, changed their infrastructure, or experienced turnover in their own security team. The framework expects continuous risk management, not a one-time gate.
