# Emerging Technology Controls: AI, Cloud, and DLT

**Reference:** CBN Risk-Based Cybersecurity Framework, Emerging Technology Provisions
**Applies To:** Commercial banks, merchant banks, non-interest banks, and Payment Service Banks
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## Why This Section Exists

Unlike many cybersecurity frameworks that leave emerging technology as an afterthought, the CBN framework explicitly names artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and distributed ledger technology as areas requiring specific risk management. This reflects how quickly Nigerian financial institutions have adopted these technologies, often faster than governance has kept pace.

---

## Artificial Intelligence

| Requirement | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| AI system inventory | Every AI tool in use, including those adopted informally by staff, should be identified |
| Risk assessment for AI use cases | Credit scoring, fraud detection, and customer service AI each carry different risk profiles requiring separate assessment |
| Data governance for AI | Understand what data feeds AI systems and whether that data is used to train models |
| Human oversight | High-stakes decisions (credit approval, fraud flags) should not be made by AI without meaningful human review |

### Practical Self-Check

- Does your institution know every AI tool currently in use, including tools adopted by individual teams without formal procurement?
- Can you explain, in plain language, how your AI-driven fraud detection or credit scoring system reaches its decisions?
- If a customer disputed an AI-driven decision, could you produce a human who reviewed it?

---

## Cloud Computing

| Requirement | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| Cloud provider risk assessment | Security posture of cloud providers assessed before migration, not after |
| Shared responsibility understanding | Clear internal documentation of what the cloud provider secures versus what the institution must secure |
| Data residency and sovereignty | Understanding where customer data is physically stored and whether this satisfies regulatory expectations |
| Configuration management | Cloud misconfigurations are a leading cause of breaches; regular configuration audits are expected |

### Practical Self-Check

- Can your team clearly explain which security responsibilities belong to your cloud provider versus your own team?
- Has a configuration audit of your cloud environment been conducted in the last 12 months?
- Do you know, specifically, in which countries your customer data is stored and processed?

---

## Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)

| Requirement | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| DLT use case risk assessment | If DLT is used for any product or process, its specific risks must be separately assessed |
| Smart contract review | Where smart contracts are used, code review and security testing should be documented |
| Regulatory alignment | DLT use must be assessed against broader CBN and SEC guidance on digital assets, where applicable |

### Practical Self-Check

- If your institution does not currently use DLT, is this explicitly documented as out of scope, or simply unaddressed?
- If DLT is used anywhere in your operations, has it been risk-assessed with the same rigour as traditional systems?

---

## Building This Into Your Risk Register

Emerging technology risks should sit in the same enterprise risk register as traditional cybersecurity risks, not in a separate innovation or IT-only document. This ensures the Board sees AI, cloud, and DLT risk with the same visibility as any other enterprise risk.

| Risk Category | Example Entry | Likely Owner |
|---|---|---|
| AI governance gap | Customer-facing AI chatbot processes personal data with no documented data flow | Head of Compliance |
| Cloud misconfiguration | Publicly accessible cloud storage bucket containing customer records | CISO / Cloud Security Lead |
| DLT smart contract risk | Unaudited smart contract handling customer transactions | Head of Technology |
