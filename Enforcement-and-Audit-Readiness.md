# Enforcement and Audit Readiness

**Reference:** CBN Risk-Based Cybersecurity Framework, Enforcement Provisions
**Applies To:** Commercial banks, merchant banks, non-interest banks, and Payment Service Banks
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## How the CBN Enforces This Framework

Unlike frameworks that exist primarily as guidance, the CBN framework is backed by active, structured enforcement mechanisms. Understanding these helps institutions prepare properly rather than treating compliance as a paperwork exercise.

| Mechanism | What It Involves |
|---|---|
| Annual cybersecurity supervisory review | A structured annual review of an institution's cybersecurity posture conducted by the CBN |
| Risk-based examinations | Examinations that may be triggered by risk indicators, incident history, or as part of routine supervisory rotation |
| Industry-wide compliance audits | Sector-wide audits assessing compliance levels across multiple institutions |
| Spot checks | Unscheduled reviews that can occur without extensive advance notice |

---

## What Examiners Typically Look For

Based on the framework's structure, examination focus areas typically include:

| Focus Area | What Is Assessed |
|---|---|
| Governance evidence | Board minutes discussing cybersecurity, CISO reporting structure, approved policies |
| Risk assessment documentation | Evidence of a genuine, recent risk assessment process, not a template filled in once |
| Vendor risk records | Vendor inventories, assessment records, contract clauses |
| Incident history and response | Documentation of any incidents, how they were handled, and lessons learned |
| Technical control evidence | Proof of MFA enforcement, encryption, vulnerability management, not just policy statements |
| Training records | Evidence that staff have received cybersecurity awareness training |

## Building an Audit-Ready Evidence Folder

Institutions that perform well under examination typically maintain organised, readily accessible evidence rather than scrambling to produce documentation on request.

**Recommended folder structure:**

| Folder | Contents |
|---|---|
| 01_Governance | Board-approved policy, CISO appointment letter, reporting structure documentation, Board meeting minutes referencing cybersecurity |
| 02_Risk_Management | Risk assessment reports, risk register extracts, risk treatment plans |
| 03_Vendor_Risk | Vendor inventory, assessment records, sample contracts with security clauses |
| 04_Technical_Controls | Configuration evidence, vulnerability scan reports, penetration test reports, patch management logs |
| 05_Incident_Response | Incident response plan, incident logs, post-incident review reports |
| 06_Training | Training completion records, security awareness materials |
| 07_Emerging_Technology | AI, cloud, and DLT risk assessments |
| 08_Prior_Examination_Findings | Previous CBN findings and evidence of remediation |

## Common Reasons Institutions Struggle at Examination

| Issue | Why It Happens |
|---|---|
| Documentation exists but is scattered | Evidence lives in individual staff members' inboxes or personal drives rather than a central, organised repository |
| Policies exist but are not followed | A well-written policy with no evidence of actual implementation raises more concern than a simpler policy that is genuinely followed |
| Risk assessments are stale | A risk assessment from two years ago, never updated, does not reflect current risk |
| No evidence of Board engagement | Cybersecurity policies approved by management alone, with no Board minutes referencing discussion or approval |
| Remediation of prior findings is undocumented | Previous examination findings addressed informally with no paper trail showing closure |

## Pre-Examination Self-Assessment

Before an anticipated review, work through this quick readiness check:

| # | Question | Ready? |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Can you produce your current Board-approved cybersecurity policy within minutes of being asked? | |
| 2 | Can you show a risk assessment conducted within the last 12 months? | |
| 3 | Can you produce your complete vendor inventory with risk tiers assigned? | |
| 4 | Can you show evidence of MFA enforcement across privileged accounts? | |
| 5 | Can you produce training completion records for all staff? | |
| 6 | If you have had a prior CBN finding, can you show documented evidence of remediation? | |
| 7 | Can you demonstrate Board-level engagement with cybersecurity risk through meeting minutes? | |

If any answer is no, that is your priority action item before your next review cycle.
